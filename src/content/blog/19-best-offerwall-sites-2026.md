## What Is an Offerwall?

An **offerwall** is a panel of third-party earning tasks — surveys, app installs, sign-ups, game achievements — embedded inside a rewards platform. You browse available tasks, complete one, and earn points or coins redeemable for vouchers or cash.

The name comes from the visual layout: a wall of available offers displayed together. Behind the scenes, the offerwall you see inside an app is powered by a specialist network that manages advertiser relationships and handles payout verification.

## What Makes an Offerwall Network Reliable?

**Server-to-server postbacks:** Reliable networks use direct server-to-server notifications to confirm and credit completions — not client-side tracking that can be blocked by ad blockers or lost if you close your browser early.

**Established advertiser base:** Networks with real, established advertising clients have deeper and more consistent offer inventories. A thin advertiser base means fewer offers and more frequent "nothing available" gaps.

**Fraud detection calibration:** The best networks have fraud detection that catches genuinely fraudulent activity without over-flagging honest respondents. Poor calibration means legitimate completions get reversed.

**Track record:** Networks that have operated for years and maintain integrations with major earning platforms have a proven track record that newer, untested networks don't.

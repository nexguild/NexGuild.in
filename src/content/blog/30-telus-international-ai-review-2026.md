## What Is Telus International AI and How It Works

Telus International AI is a division of Telus International, a Canadian technology company. The AI division specifically handles what the industry calls "data annotation" and "AI training data" work — the human review processes that make AI models more accurate, safe, and useful. Their clients include major search engines, social media companies, and AI developers who need large volumes of human judgment applied to their data at scale.

To generate this judgment at scale, Telus International AI maintains a global network of independent contractors called "crowd members." These are people working from home, on their own schedule, completing tasks through a web-based platform. The tasks range from evaluating search engine results to reviewing social media content, verifying map data, and testing AI assistants.

Getting started involves submitting an application through the Telus International AI website. Unlike some crowdsourcing platforms where you can start working within hours of signing up, Telus International AI has a structured intake process. You create a profile, provide information about your language skills and location, and wait to be matched with projects that fit your background. If a suitable project is available in your region, you will typically be invited to complete a qualification process before accessing paid work.

## The Task Types Available on Telus International AI

The most widely available and well-known work on Telus International AI is search engine rating, also called search quality rating or web research evaluation. In this work, you receive a search query — the type of phrase a real person might type into Google or another search engine — and you evaluate whether the results that engine returned genuinely serve what the user was trying to accomplish. You then apply a detailed rating scale, documenting your judgment according to strict guidelines.

Other task categories available through Telus International AI include:

**Social media content rating** — reviewing posts, comments, and ads on social platforms for quality, relevance, and policy compliance. These projects often run alongside major platform review initiatives.

**Map data evaluation** — verifying the accuracy of location information, business listings, and geographic data used in navigation and mapping products.

**AI output evaluation** — reviewing responses generated by AI assistants, chatbots, or generative tools and rating them for accuracy, helpfulness, and safety. This category has grown significantly as demand for AI training data has expanded.

**Web research tasks** — structured information gathering, where you look up specific facts, verify business details, or collect data from web sources according to defined templates.

**Data annotation** — labeling images, text, or audio clips to build training datasets for machine learning models.

Project availability varies significantly by country, language, and time period. Search engine rating projects are the most consistently available globally, but they open and close based on client demand.

## The Qualification Process: What to Expect

The qualification process at Telus International AI is one of the most discussed — and most challenging — aspects of the platform. For search quality rating specifically, passing the qualification requires demonstrating that you have genuinely understood the Search Quality Rater Guidelines, a document that runs over 160 pages and defines exactly how to evaluate web content and search results across dozens of scenarios.

The process typically involves studying these guidelines thoroughly on your own, then completing a multi-part exam that presents you with real search scenarios and asks you to apply the rating framework correctly. The exam is not multiple-choice memorization — it requires applying nuanced judgment to ambiguous cases, exactly the way the guidelines instruct.

Many applicants fail the first attempt, especially those who try to rush through the guidelines rather than reading them carefully. The exam is designed to catch surface-level familiarity, not deep understanding. Contractors who have passed typically report spending one to three full days studying before attempting the assessment.

If you do not pass, you can usually reapply after a waiting period. The threshold for passing has tightened over time as the platform has grown more selective about its contractor base.

For other project types — map evaluation, AI output rating, web research — the qualification requirements are generally less intensive, though each project has its own standards and training materials.

## Pay Rates: What Telus International AI Actually Pays

Pay rates on Telus International AI vary considerably depending on the project type, your location, and the specific client funding the work. For search quality rating, the most common project type, rates for contributors in English-speaking markets typically fall between $12 and $18 per hour equivalent. Some specialized projects — particularly those requiring rare language skills or domain expertise — pay more.

An important nuance: Telus International AI typically pays per task completed, not a flat hourly rate. The hourly figure you see is an estimate based on average completion time for that task type. If you work efficiently and develop familiarity with the rating guidelines, your effective rate may exceed the estimate. Early on, when each task takes longer than average, your effective rate will be lower.

The platform also sets weekly hour caps on most projects — often between 20 and 30 hours, sometimes lower. This reflects the reality that AI training data work is structured as a supplemental income source for most participants, not a full-time arrangement.

Payment is processed through a dedicated payment portal, and the schedule varies by country — some regions receive weekly payments, others bi-weekly or monthly. PayPal is the most common payment method for international contributors.

## Telus International AI vs Appen: Key Differences

Telus International AI and Appen are the two most prominent names in the search quality rating and AI training data space, and contributors frequently compare them. Both offer similar work, similar pay ranges, and similar independent contractor arrangements. The differences are more about experience and stability than fundamental structure.

Appen has historically been better known for its breadth of task types beyond search rating — particularly image annotation, audio transcription, and data collection projects. Telus International AI has a stronger reputation specifically for search quality rating and social media evaluation, with more structured project management and more consistent communication with contractors.

Appen pays monthly, which is one of the most common complaints on that platform. Telus International AI's more frequent payment schedules in many regions are a meaningful practical advantage. On the other hand, Appen's global contractor network is larger, which can mean more project opportunities in some regions.

Most experienced contributors in this space recommend maintaining accounts on both platforms. Project availability fluctuates with client contracts, and having two sources of search rating work protects your income when one platform enters a quiet period. ## The Genuine Downsides

**Getting in is unpredictable.** Some applicants receive a project invitation within days of applying; others wait months or never hear back. This depends entirely on whether a matching project is currently open in your language and region, and Telus International AI does not always communicate clearly about the status of your application.

**Work volume is not guaranteed.** Even once you qualify, project availability fluctuates based on client contracts. A project you relied on for 25 hours per week can be paused or ended with little notice. This is not unique to Telus International AI — it is structural to how AI training data work is funded — but it means the platform cannot function as a predictable sole income source.

**The qualification exam is a real investment.** Spending two to three days studying a 160-page document before earning your first dollar is a real time cost. If you fail and have to wait to reapply, that investment can feel significant. The pay rates justify this cost for contributors who pass, but it is worth knowing going in.

**Support is slow.** Like most platforms in this space, Telus International AI's contractor support is handled through email tickets, and response times are often measured in days. For most day-to-day questions the documentation is sufficient, but when something goes wrong with a payment or account, resolution takes time.
